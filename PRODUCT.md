# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Local small-business owners — cafes, gyms and PTs, trades, clinics, coaches. They land here from a cold or warm email, usually on a phone, mid-day, distracted. Not technical, not "into" websites; they judge in seconds on feel. Time-poor and a little burned by agencies or template farms. The job to be done: decide, fast, that this studio makes genuinely high-end custom sites, is run by a real person, will make them money, and is worth a reply or a call.

## Product Purpose
MH Studio is a one-person web-design studio (founder: Miller Hughes) that builds custom, fast-shipped "AI-era" websites for local businesses — a one-off build plus a monthly care plan. This single-page site is the studio's whole shopfront and its primary proof: the page itself is the portfolio piece. Success = a recipient lands, believes the quality is real, understands the money case (leads, trust, revenue, time back), trusts the method for getting them found (SEO/AEO), and books a call or replies. It carries the wedge offer so the umbrella brand need not be settled to start selling.

## Brand Personality
Precise, crafted, confident, warm, direct. The voice of a skilled maker who shows the work rather than talks about it — plain English, no jargon, no hype, no filler. Three words: **true, made, fast.** Emotional goal: the quiet confidence of something built properly — a well-made object, not a sales funnel. A time-poor owner should feel "this person is sharp, low-drama, and will get it done and look after me."

## Anti-references
- Faceless agency sites: gradient blobs, stock "team" photos, vague "we deliver digital solutions" copy.
- Template-farm / Wix-tier landing pages: generic hero, identical feature cards, no point of view.
- Dark-mode developer aesthetic: neon-on-black, terminal cosplay.
- Cream/sand editorial-magazine look: warm-paper bg, italic display serif, drop caps, tracked-caps eyebrows on every section.
- Inflated marketing: unverifiable statistics, "10x your revenue," countdown-timer urgency, hollow superlatives.

## Design Principles
- **The page is the portfolio.** Every pixel is the strongest proof of the offer; if the site isn't impeccable, the pitch is dead.
- **Show the work, honestly.** Demo builds are labelled as demo builds. No invented clients, testimonials, or fabricated result stats — credibility must survive the first reply. Method over promises while pre-revenue; real numbers get added once real sites produce them.
- **Money case over feature list.** Frame everything as outcome for the owner — leads, trust, revenue, time back — not as things Miller does.
- **Made by a person.** Founder-direct warmth beats agency polish; one human builds, ships, and looks after it.
- **Fast is a feature.** The brand sells speed; the page should feel quick, decisive, and light. Respect the reader's time the way the offer promises to.
- **Structural, not decorative.** The proof-mark device (crop ticks, measurement ticks, blueprint grid) is used as real registration marks, never as ornament.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1; forest-teal `--primary` used as accent / large / white-on-primary only, never as small body text on a tinted surface (see DESIGN.md contrast notes). Full keyboard support and visible focus. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback (instant/crossfade). Content is visible by default, never gated on a JS reveal. Mobile-first: most traffic arrives on a phone from an email link.
